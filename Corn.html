Steps to Growing Organic Corn:


1. Choose a Variety: Select a corn variety suited to your climate and purpose (sweet corn, popcorn, field corn). Sweet corn varieties are best for eating fresh.
2. Prepare the Soil: Corn thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. Amend with plenty of compost or aged manure. Corn is a heavy feeder.
3. Plant Seeds: Sow seeds about 1 inch deep and 6-12 inches apart in rows. Plant multiple rows for good pollination. Corn is wind-pollinated, so planting in blocks rather than single rows is recommended.
4. Thin Seedlings (if necessary): If you planted seeds closer together, thin seedlings to the recommended spacing after they have a few leaves.
5. Water Regularly: Corn needs consistent moisture, especially during tasseling and silking. Water deeply, especially during dry periods.
6. Weed Control: Keep the area around the corn plants free of weeds, especially when they are young. Mulching can help suppress weeds.
7. Fertilizing: Corn benefits from side-dressings of compost tea or a balanced organic fertilizer during the growing season.
8. Pest and Disease Control: Watch for pests like corn earworms and corn borers. Use organic pest control methods if necessary. Ensure good air circulation to minimize fungal diseases.
9. Harvest: Harvest sweet corn when the silks are brown and the kernels are plump. Popcorn is harvested when the kernels are dry and hard. Field corn is typically left to dry in the field before harvesting.
