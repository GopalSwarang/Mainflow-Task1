Steps to Growing Organic Onions:


1. Choose a Variety: Select an onion variety suited to your climate and desired use (e.g., short-day, long-day, intermediate-day). Consider storage potential as well.
2. Prepare the Soil: Onions need loose, well-drained soil rich in organic matter. Amend with plenty of compost or aged manure. Avoid freshly applied manure. A soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0 is ideal.
3. Start Seeds Indoors (Recommended): Starting onion seeds indoors 8-10 weeks before the last expected frost is recommended for most regions. This allows for better development before transplanting.
4. Transplant Seedlings: Transplant onion seedlings outdoors when they are about 4-6 inches tall and the danger of frost has passed. Plant them 4-6 inches apart in rows, with rows spaced 1-2 feet apart.
5. Water Regularly: Onions need consistent moisture, especially during bulb development. Water deeply but less frequently rather than shallowly and often.
6. Weed Control: Keep the area around the onion plants free of weeds, especially when they are young. Mulching can help suppress weeds and retain moisture.
7. Fertilizing: Onions benefit from side-dressings of compost tea or a balanced organic fertilizer during the growing season.
8. Pest and Disease Control: Watch for pests like onion thrips and onion maggots. Use organic pest control methods if necessary. Onions can also be susceptible to diseases like bulb rot. Good air circulation and crop rotation can help prevent problems.
9. Harvest: Harvest onions when the tops have fallen over and the bulbs are mature. Allow the onions to dry in the sun for a few days before storing them in a cool, dry place.
