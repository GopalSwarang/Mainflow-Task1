Steps to Growing Organic Carrots:


1. Prepare the Soil: Carrots need loose, well-drained soil free of rocks and clumps. Amend with compost or aged manure. Avoid fresh manure. A sandy or loamy soil is ideal.
2. Choose a Variety: Select carrot varieties suitable for your climate and desired size (e.g., Nantes, Imperator, Chantenay).
3. Sow Seeds: Plant seeds ¼ inch deep and ½ inch apart in rows. Thin seedlings later. Carrot seeds are tiny, so even distribution can be challenging. Seed tapes or pre-seeded mats can help.
4. Thin Seedlings: Once seedlings have a few true leaves, thin them to 2-3 inches apart. This is crucial for proper root development.
5. Water Regularly: Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during germination and early growth. Avoid overwatering, which can lead to rot.
6. Weed and Mulch: Remove weeds regularly. Mulching helps retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7. Fertilize Sparingly: Carrots generally don't need heavy fertilization. A side-dressing of compost tea or a balanced organic fertilizer can be used if needed. Avoid nitrogen-rich fertilizers, as they can cause forking.
8. Control Pests/Diseases: Watch for pests like carrot flies and aphids. Use organic pest control methods if necessary. Rotate crops to minimize disease problems.
9. Harvest: Harvest carrots when they reach the desired size. Loosen the soil around the carrots before pulling them out by the greens.
