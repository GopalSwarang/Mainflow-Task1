Steps to Growing Organic Cabbage:


1. Choose a Variety: Select a cabbage variety suitable for your climate and growing season (e.g., early, mid-season, late). Consider varieties like 'Green Acre', 'Savoy', or 'Red Acre'.
2. Prepare the Soil: Cabbage prefers fertile, well-drained soil rich in organic matter. Amend with compost or aged manure. Cabbage also prefers a soil pH between 6.0 and 7.0.
3. Start Seeds Indoors (Recommended): Start cabbage seeds indoors 4-6 weeks before the last expected frost. This gives them a head start. Transplant seedlings outdoors after the danger of frost has passed.
4. Transplant Seedlings: Plant seedlings 12-24 inches apart in rows, depending on the variety. Space rows 2-3 feet apart.
5. Water Regularly: Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during head formation. Cabbage needs consistent moisture for proper growth.
6. Weed Control: Regularly remove weeds, especially when the plants are young. Mulching can help retain moisture and suppress weeds.
7. Fertilizing: Cabbage benefits from side-dressings of compost tea or a balanced organic fertilizer during the growing season.
8. Pest and Disease Control: Watch for pests like cabbage worms, aphids, and slugs. Use organic pest control methods if necessary. Cabbage can also be susceptible to diseases like clubroot. Crop rotation can help prevent this.
9. Harvest: Harvest cabbage when the heads are firm and solid. Cut the head off at the base, leaving a few outer leaves.
